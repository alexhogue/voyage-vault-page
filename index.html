Voyage Vault

Create your dream travel bucket list!

Overview

The goal of the project was to design and build an interface for users to create their dream travel bucket list, share their travel experiences, and explore others' travel opinions.

The final product is a fully functional interactive interface that displays travel destinations to users, allows them to sort and filter the options, and add their favorites to their "vault" which functions as an aggregator to display users' selections "vault".

Tools:

Figma, ReactJS, Procreate

Preperation

I began the design process by analyzing and drawing comparisons between various existing interfaces that implement a favoriting feature in order to figure out the clearest and most effective way to implement my travel bucket list builder.

Importance of visual layout

Based on the Competitive Comparison chart, it is clearly important that a favorite function be intuitive and clear to users. This includes how the items are displayed visually on the interface. The aspects that were most successful between these three interfaces include displaying many options to the user directly on the main page so that a user can scroll through the options without having to click to other pages. Netflix and TripAdvisor do this, whereas Goodreads requires a user to click on a genre before viewing all options. Forcing users to make choices can be less effective than letting them browse freely. All three sites employ a similar visual structure that works effectively; a grided layout of options creates visual hierarchy and use of repeated forms that clearly communicate their function and orient the user on the page.

Importance of intuitive flow

Making the favorite action intuitive is equally important; TripAdvisor uses a simple heart icon, whereas Netflix requires hover for the like option to appear, and Goodreads has numerous like/save/rating options that may be too wordy and confusing for a user. Additionally, clear “unliking” is important to allow users flexibility to modify their lists or rectify errors; Netflix does this very clearly by simply allowing users to click the like button again to unlike it, whereas Goodreads and TripAdvisor require additional steps. All three sites provided the user with effective feedback when liking items to communicate a user’s completed and successful action.


Based on my analysis, it is important to implement the following factors in my own design to optimize the user experience with the interface:

  • Clear layout with all options viewable on the home page
  • Allow sorting by category
  • Limit excess wordiness
  • Use clear icons for liking items without extra clicks needed
  • Easy removal of items once added

Design

Planning

I began by planning out my design using Figma with a focus on clarity and accessibility.

Using the information gleaned from my competitive comparison, I took the elements from the three interfaces that were most effective, and revised elements that were less effective.

Design Choices

Layout

The purpose of the app is to provide users with travel destination ideas and allow them to build their travel bucket list and view other users opinions and rankings. It is that the travel information for each destination be clearly layed out into item cards for users to easily browse.

Sort and Filter

Because travel is incredibly distinct to each individual's preferences, sorting and filtering the options is extremely useful. I created the options for users to sort by most liked destinations and filter destinations by both continent as well as desired activity (e.g city, beach, and adventure focused destinations).

Aggregator

The user is then able to add their favorite destinations to their travel vault. The vault is the aggregator that displays the users choices in a section to the left of the travel grid so that users can easily view and update their list as they browse.

Illustrations

Additionally, I created colorful illustrations to make the page more visually engaging and to capture the excitment and discovery of travel.

Key Takeaways

Clear and Accessible Layout: Providing a clear layout with all options readily viewable enhances usability and allows users to easily navigate and interact with the interface.

Simplified Interaction: Using clear icons and limiting excess wordiness helps streamline user interactions and reduces cognitive load, resulting in a more efficient and enjoyable user experience.

Flexibility and Feedback: Offering options for sorting, favoriting, and removing items, along with clear feedback mechanisms, empowers users to customize their experience and provides valuable feedback on their actions.
